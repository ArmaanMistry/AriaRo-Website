1. PICK SWITCH

A unique and eccentric game for coders who like to take on challenges. Every participant is to flaunt their ability of coding by finishing an anonymous code that is halfway done by another participant.

Rules:

  1. Participants must finish the code by themselves. No online compilers or helping measures can be used.
  2. No unfair means will be accepted. If caught person or team will be disqualified.

2. ROBO RACE

Robo race is an event planned for race fans and geeks. Every robot fan dream come true where the teams must use their knowledge in the robotics field and make their own robot for this competition.

Rules:

  1. No unfair means will be accepted. If caught, the team will be disqualified, and strict actions will be taken.
  2. No weapons would be allowed to be fitted on the robots.
  3. All students with a valid identity card of their respective educational institutes are eligible to participate in the event.
  4. The autonomous bot must be completely autonomous with just one switch to start reset it and to calibrate the line follower.
  5. The bot should not damage the arena when running or performing any task. It should also not damage the line of the line following path. If someone is found doing so, the team will be disqualified or penalized. The decisions rest on the organizers and will be final and binding for all.

3. ROBO FIGHT

A thrilling event that will push the participants and the audience on the edges of their seats. Participants are supposed to bring robots created by them (individually) or by their team.

Rules:

  1. Participation: All participants build and operate Robots at their own risk. Robot Wars is inherently dangerous.
  2. Event Rules: It is expected that competitors stay within the rules and procedures of their own accord and do not require constant policing.
  3. Safety Inspections: It is at the inspector’s sole discretion that your robot is allowed to compete. As a builder you are obligated to disclose all operating principles and potential dangers to the inspection staff.
  4. Cardinal Safety Rules: Failure to comply with any of the cardinal safety rules set out below by Robot Wars may result in expulsion from the event or worse, injury and death.
  5. Weight Limit: The maximum weight limit allowed is 110kg.
  6. Activation:
    • Activation: Proper activation and deactivation of robots is critical.
    • Weapon Restraints: All Robots not in an arena or official testing area should have secure safety covers over any sharp edges and restraints on any active weapons or pinch hazards.
    • Restrictions: In some situations, the safety inspection team may deem it necessary to place restrictions on your robot’s operation for safety purposes.
    • Power Tools: The use of welders, grinders and other equipment that may produce smoke, debris or other harmful substances is only permitted in dedicated workshop areas.

4. TECH TREASURE

A treasure that does not need excavating but solved, a game of treasure hunt with a twist. Instead of common riddles, the participants will be provided with a code that will give them their clue in forms of an output. Bonus pts codes, trojan code. TEAM REGISTRATIONS

Rules:

  1. Participants must decode the given codes by themselves.
  2. No online compilers or helping measures can be used.
  3. No unfair means will be accepted. If caught person or team will be disqualified.

5. DEBATE ON CYBERLAW & DARK WEB

Trying to solve the huge debate happening on the topics of cyberlaw and dark web. This event is organized to conduct a heated debate on the topic.

Rules:

  1. No harsh language will be tolerated.
  2. No arguments against the jury.

6. CODE-A-THON

A coding marathon where participants are supposed to be solving a problem in each time. This will also show their technical expertise in competitive environment.

Rules:

  1. Participants must solve the problem by themselves. No online compilers or helping measures can be used.
  2. No unfair means will be accepted. If caught person or team will be disqualified.
  3. Internet usage is strictly prohibited.
